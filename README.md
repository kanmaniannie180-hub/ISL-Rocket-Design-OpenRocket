# 🚀 Single-Stage Model Rocket Design and Flight Simulation using OpenRocket

## Overview

This project focuses on the design, aerodynamic analysis, stability evaluation, and flight simulation of a single-stage model rocket using **OpenRocket**. The objective was to develop a stable and efficient rocket configuration capable of achieving high-altitude flight while maintaining safe recovery characteristics.

The project involved rocket geometry design, motor selection, stability assessment, trajectory prediction, and performance analysis through multiple simulation scenarios. Various motor configurations were evaluated to study their impact on altitude, velocity, acceleration, flight duration, and recovery performance.

---

## Objectives

* Design a stable single-stage model rocket.
* Analyze aerodynamic characteristics and flight behavior.
* Evaluate rocket stability using Center of Gravity (CG) and Center of Pressure (CP) relationships.
* Compare performance across different motor configurations.
* Estimate maximum altitude, velocity, and acceleration.
* Assess recovery system effectiveness and landing safety.
* Gain practical experience in aerospace design and simulation tools.

* Center of Gravity (CG)
* Center of Pressure (CP)

The design maintained a positive stability margin throughout all simulations, ensuring:

* Straight flight path

## Flight Simulation Analysis

The simulation results demonstrated that motor selection significantly influences overall rocket performance.

Key observations include:

* Increased motor thrust resulted in higher apogee values.
* Higher-powered motors generated greater acceleration and velocity.
* The rocket remained aerodynamically stable under all tested conditions.
* Recovery system performance remained satisfactory across all configurations.
* High-thrust configurations achieved near-supersonic flight speeds.
